## **What is a chatbot?**

At its core, a chatbot is software that interacts with users through conversation.

### **Traditional chatbots**

Traditional chatbots are rule-based. They operate on predefined conversation flows. If you ask something unexpected or phrase a question differently, they may get confused or fail to respond meaningfully. These bots are best for predictable, structured tasks. For example, if you build a chatbot to help users reset their password, it might follow this flow:

1. Ask for username
    
2. Validate identity
    
3. Provide reset link
    

However, if a user asks a question outside of the expected pattern—like “Can I use my old password?”—the bot might fail to respond properly. These bots are best for:

- Handling repetitive, structured tasks
    
- Guiding users through forms or surveys
    
- Simple customer support
    

They require developers to write expected _intents_ (goals), _utterances_ (phrases), and response logic manually.

### **AI-powered chatbots**

AI chatbots are powered by machine learning and foundation models (FMs), which allow them to understand natural language, maintain context, and generate human-like responses—even to questions they weren’t explicitly trained for. AI chatbots offer huge advantages over traditional bots:

- They scale effortlessly with foundation models trained on massive datasets
    
- They personalize answers based on history or context
    
- They evolve—no need to manually script every possible phrase
    
- They integrate with external data, knowledge bases, and APIs
    

## **What can AI chatbots do?**

AI chatbots are capable of:

- Answering customer and employee questions
    
- Searching and summarizing information from internal documents
    
- Guiding users through decisions or processes
    
- Translating languages or summarizing legal documents
    
- Providing personalized, context-aware support
    

For example, an AI chatbot can summarize a company’s travel policy, and then help a user book a compliant trip based on destination, budget, and preferences.
## **How are AI chatbots different from AI agents?**

Sometimes the line between an AI chatbot and an AI agent gets blurry. So how do we tell them apart? Think of them this way:

- AI chatbots are good for conversational tasks and light automation.
    
- AI agents can perform multi-step tasks, reason through decisions, and handle complex logic.
