## Natural Language Processing (NLP)

**Description:**  
Natural Language Processing (NLP) is a subfield of Artificial Intelligence that helps computers understand, interpret, and generate human language.  
It bridges the gap between human communication and machine understanding.

**Example:**  
Applications include chatbots, speech recognition (like Siri or Alexa), and tools like Grammarly that check grammar and tone.

**Remember:**  
NLP = **AI + Linguistics + Computer Science**  
It deals with **both speech and text data**.

## Components of NLP

**Description:**  
NLP consists of several stages that help computers process natural language step-by-step.

### **Lexical Analysis**

**Description:**  
Analyzing the structure of words — splitting sentences into tokens (words or phrases).  
**Example:**  
Sentence: “ChatGPT explains NLP.”  
→ Tokens: ["ChatGPT", "explains", "NLP"]

**Remember:**  
Tokenization and lemmatization are part of this step.

### **Syntactic Analysis (Parsing)**

**Description:**  
Examines the grammatical structure of a sentence.  
Checks how words relate to each other using grammar rules.

**Example:**  
“ChatGPT explains NLP clearly.”  
→ Subject: ChatGPT, Verb: explains, Object: NLP

**Remember:**  
It ensures sentences are grammatically correct and meaningful.

### **Semantic Analysis**

**Description:**  
Focuses on understanding the _meaning_ of the words and sentences.  
It goes beyond syntax to interpret context.

**Example:**  
“The bank of the river” ≠ “The bank account” — same word, different meanings.

**Remember:**  
Deals with word sense disambiguation and meaning representation.

### Tokenization

**Description:**  
Splits text into smaller units called _tokens_ — words, subwords, or sentences.

**Example:**  
Sentence: “Natural Language Processing is fun.”  
→ Tokens: ["Natural", "Language", "Processing", "is", "fun"]

**Remember:**  
Tokenization is the **first step** in most NLP pipelines.
